About Kostanay

Kostanay is a city in northern Kazakhstan, located on the Tobol River. Founded in the 19th century, it has grown into a significant cultural and economic center in the region. With a population of approximately 220,000 people, Kostanay is known for its rich history, including its role as a key agricultural hub, and its diverse architecture reflecting Russian and Kazakh influences. The city features beautiful parks, museums, and landmarks such as the Regional Museum of Local History and the Kostanay Arch. Today, it serves as an important educational and industrial center, with a vibrant community life.
